Scale at speed bootcamp

Our clients need to stand out and keep ahead. Through our bootcamp they have built fast, iterative software delivery capability and have grown an adaptive, innovation culture throughout the organisation. We loved working with them and want to do the same for you

To do this we look for them to:

  • Engage

    As teams and tech stacks become more complex, collaboration becomes more critical. Teams require everyone to behave well—logically, skillfully and consistently. Let's be willing and ready to listen, ask and respond; let's have a social contract to be active and proactive.

  • Act

    Technology change can be overwhelming in complex or even chaotic situations. That's when it is even more important to act small and to probe the situation and learn from how it responds. Only then will we know if what we did was right.

  • Explain

    As tech stack complexity grows, organising teams around technology layers becomes less and less effective. As a consequence everyone needs to have a clearer, wider and deeper understanding. In the end, it is about a willingness to clarify, simplify and collaborate to promote shared understanding. We can do this in a variety of ways: write documents, draw diagrams, speak and summarise for different audiences.

  • Learn

    We need to meet the challenge of building out our breadth and depth of technology skills. But being able to adapt and learn is as important as building specific technical skills. When we are in the middle of innovation let's learn from others mistakes and ensure we don't continue making the same mistakes time and time again!

Engagement Process.

Our clients put their trust in us, so we hold ourselves to the highest standards to ensure that they are building world-competing software capability.

Our process takes 3 steps:

  • Discussion

    A series of meetings as we get to know your business and you get to know us. We need to have a sense of your business model, your current capability and where the opportunities to us to add value really lie.

  • Agreement

    Talking money and timeframes is really important. We like to be upfront about what we think is likely and about what we expect of ourselves and of our clients.

  • Preparation

    We want to make the best use of both your and our time and we find that there is some prep work to be done. Your teams are going need to spend time preparing around certain technologies well us throughout bootcamp. We'll also need to confirm many of the issues raised in the earlier discussions.

Key Approach.

Once we have agreed to start, we target complexity that can be simplified—we break it up into manageable parts. For us, world-competitive software capability requires full-stack technologists who understand the flow of work and can navigate the tradeoffs between short, mid and long-term business goals. And the baseline of this is that you must be able to build and deploy from source through a pipeline, and fast through every stage. Transitioning all this takes time, patience, courage and a single mindedness, plus a little bit of having been there before. So, we lead by example, guided by social coding and reinforced through coaching.

Boardroom to keyboard.

We've left the best to last. Change like this isn't effective unless it is viral throughout the organisation. We know that it needs to come from the boardroom and the keyboard at the same time; and our role to ensure that the two can find a common language. We help the board to understand how to interpret the new information that is coming from build pipeline and ensure that their requests are grounded in the reality of what is possible as well as dream of the possible. We work at the keyboard to ensure that the team have the capability to respond to the boardroom requests.

  • When to Start Talking

    2-8 weeks before you intend to start.

    For bootcamp, we recommend that you start early on discussions so that we can reach an agreement on how to proceed and give your team as much warning as possible.

  • Prep course

    Length: 1-4 weeks

    We will provide materials that can be started on at your own pace. Some work is new material to learn, other parts are ensuring that there is right infrastructure for when we arrive. We will also be doing remote work to ensure that we have a clear picture.

  • Bootcamp

    Length: 1-3 months in 1 week cycles

    We will co-work with your team through a mixture of onsite and remote working. For each week, we ensure there is strategic alignment in the prioritisation of work across: features, technical debt, production triage and process improvements. Throughout the week, we work in a daily delivery cycle of discuss-estimate-act-reestimate-communicate-demo-improve. Each and every day we will see changes going through our pipeline and see improvements to our overall systems.

    We don’t want to run bootcamp longer than needed. The purpose of bootcamp is to have a chance to work on team, tools and planning while still delivering product. We’re thinking this work is weighted 80/20 respectively. We’ll know you’re ready to graduate when we are ready flip to 80% of work on product—at that stage, we can repeatedly and reliably plan and ship, each and every day.

  • Demo Day

    Final Week

    It's time to take stock and glory in how far we have come. We show what we have done and also reflect on where we need to be when we take next step.
