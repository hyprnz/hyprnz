Scale Fail.

Our clients put their trust in us, so we hold ourselves to the highest standards to ensure that they are building world-competing software capability. Scale at Speed ensures that great ideas can get their day in the sun. And when you’re seeing genuine ‘jump-at-it-ness’ from customers, that the product can scale to keep pace with exponential demand.

This scaling is not achieved by hiring more and more developers or running up more cloud servers. It’s about keeping things simple and acting as one. Simple like 80% less code. Acting as one like frequent, brief check-ins with cross-functional groups to get decisions made quickly and keep work moving. Essentially, we’re working from the boardroom to the keyboard to help you avoid ‘scale fail’—an issue affecting too many NZ tech companies.

  • Pace of delivery slows

    Initial releases (MVPs) are necessarily “fast and cheap”. But often, when it comes time to grow, delivery starts to take twice as long and cost twice as much. The slow pace of delivery eventually puts pressure on cashflow, and the tech team becomes an unbearable cost. We call this “scale fail”.

  • Profitability potential not realised

    Ultimately, the potential profitability of the company is not realised—so the business owner loses, investors lose and the NZ economy loses.

  • Transition to meet demand

    Startups need to be able to transition from one “right-scale” to the next as demand grows.

  • Technical execution partner

    We have experience of remediating these issues before companies hit scale fail. We can rapidly help to right-scale the technical execution at the critical stage between product/market-fit validation and scaling.

Approach.

Our approach is to amplify work inside startups to avoid scale fail and unleash profit potential. This programme is most suited to small growth companies who have genuine unmet demand.

Once we have agreed to start, we target complexity that can be simplified—we break it up into manageable parts.

Our Team.

Our transition teams are made up of 2-3 people—a mix of technologists and product delivery folk.

Transitioning all this takes time, patience, courage and a single mindedness, plus a little bit of having been there before. So, we lead by example, guided by social coding and reinforced through coaching.

Boardroom to keyboard.

We've left the best to last. Change like this isn't effective unless it is viral throughout the organisation. We know that it needs to engage both the boardroom and the keyboard; and it is our role to ensure that the two can find a common language.

  • When to Start Talking

    2-8 weeks before you intend to start.

    We recommend that you start early on discussions so that we can reach an agreement on how to proceed and give your team as much lead-time as possible.

  • Prep Work—agreeing metrics

    Length: 1-4 weeks

    A lightweight healthcheck/questionnaire followed by a one-day workshop to co-create a high-level plan of the metrics we need to track against. The workshop will cover everything from strategic alignment in the prioritisation of work to agreeing ways of working together. In the run-up, we will also provide materials that can be started on at your own pace. Some work is new material to learn, other parts are ensuring that there is the right infrastructure for when we arrive.

  • Rewire

    Length: 1-3 months in 1 week cycles

    We will work with your team through a mixture of onsite and remote working.


    For each week, we ensure there is strategic alignment in the prioritisation of work across: features, technical debt, production triage and process improvements.


    Throughout the week, we work in a daily delivery cycle of discuss-estimate-act-reestimate-communicate-demo-improve. Each and every day we will see changes going through our pipeline and see improvements to our overall systems.


    We don’t want to run longer than needed. The purpose is to leave you with a simple, explainable and scalable system and way of working—that enables you to meet the next phase of your growing customer demand. We are only successful when your customers are happy.

  • Review

    Final Week (of any cycle)

    It's time to take stock and glory in how far we have come together. Let’s look back on what we have achieved and confirm that you have what you need to take the next steps.
