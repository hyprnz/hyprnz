Are you up for the challenge?

Scale at speed bootcamp

Our startup clients need to keep ahead. With our help, they have transitioned to keep pace with growing customer demand and move into new markets. We loved working with them and want to do the same for you.

To do this we look for them to:

  • Engage

    As the drive to deliver increases and tech stacks become more complex, collaboration becomes more critical. Teams are at the heart of the way we work and that requires everyone to work together. Let's be willing and ready to listen, ask and respond.

  • Act

    Change can be overwhelming in complex or even chaotic situations. That's when it is even more important to try small changes—to watch and learn from the response. Only then will we know if what we did was right.

  • Focus

    We will need you to make tough trade-off decisions to enable the team to focus on the most valuable work first. This is a dynamic process at the strategy and product roadmap level where you will negotiate short and long-term goals.

  • Learn

    We need to meet the challenge of building out our breadth and depth of technology and product development skills. But being able to adapt and learn is as important as building skills. Let’s explore new ideas and ways of working to find the right fit for your business.

In case it wasn’t clear:

It's going to be pretty intense

Engagement Process.

Our clients put their trust in us, so we hold ourselves to the highest standards to ensure that they are building world-competing software capability.

Our process takes three steps:

  • Discussion

    A few meetings as we get to know your business and you get to know us. We need to have a sense of your business model, your current capability and where the opportunities for us to add value really lie.

  • Agreement

    Talking money and timeframes is really important. We like to be upfront about what we think is likely and about what we expect of ourselves and of our clients.

  • Preparation

    We want to make the best use of both your and our time and we find that some prep work really helps.

Approach.

Once we have agreed to start, we target complexity that can be simplified—we break it up into manageable parts.

Our Team.

Our transition teams are made up of 2-3 people—a mix of technologists and product delivery folk.

Transitioning all this takes time, patience, courage and a single mindedness, plus a little bit of having been there before. So, we lead by example, guided by social coding and reinforced through coaching.

Boardroom to keyboard.

We've left the best to last. Change like this isn't effective unless it is viral throughout the organisation. We know that it needs to engage both the boardroom and the keyboard; and it is our role to ensure that the two can find a common language.

  • When to Start Talking

    2-8 weeks before you intend to start.

    For bootcamp, we recommend that you start early on discussions so that we can reach an agreement on how to proceed and give your team as much lead-time as possible.

  • Prep Work

    Length: 1-4 weeks

    A lightweight healthcheck/questionnaire followed by a one-day workshop to co-create a high-level plan for bootcamp. The workshop will cover everything from strategic alignment in the prioritisation of work to agreeing ways of working together. In the run-up to bootcamp, we will also provide materials that can be started on at your own pace. Some work is new material to learn, other parts are ensuring that there is the right infrastructure for when we arrive.

  • Bootcamp

    Length: 1-3 months in 1 week cycles

    We will work with your team through a mixture of onsite and remote working.


    For each week, we ensure there is strategic alignment in the prioritisation of work across: features, technical debt, production triage and process improvements.


    Throughout the week, we work in a daily delivery cycle of discuss-estimate-act-reestimate-communicate-demo-improve. Each and every day we will see changes going through our pipeline and see improvements to our overall systems.


    We don’t want to run bootcamp longer than needed. The purpose of bootcamp is to leave you with a simple, explainable and scalable system and way of working—that enables you to meet the next phase of your growing customer demand. We are only successful when your customers are happy.

  • Review

    Final Week

    It's time to take stock and glory in how far we have come together. Let’s look back on what we have achieved and confirm that you have what you need to take the next steps.

Money.

We work on a time-and-materials basis to a set budget and we make each and every day (and dollar) count.
